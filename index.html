I am an undergraduate researcher at the University of Washington in Seattle obtaining a double degree in computer engineering and bioengineering. As a member of the UbiComp Lab advised by Dr. Shwetak Patel, I explore applications of computing tools to improve the quality and accessibility of healthcare. My research includes prototyping mobile health systems to measure medical vital signs and risk factors, building wearable sensors to perform continuous physiological sensing, and designing computing tools to support population health and assay automation. I am fortunate to work closely with colleagues in computer science, electrical engineering, and bioengineering, in addition to several outstanding clinical collaborators from University of Washington Medicine.

Through research, courses, and independent projects, I have built skills in biosignal processing, embedded systems, circuit design, and fabrication. In fall of 2020 I will be applying to computer science PhD programs.
